# Idea Brainstrom

## Idea 1: High School Life

1. The avatar starts off the game entering into high school.  
2. There should be a back story to provide the player with the ability to make choices that affect their life.  
3. The player should be given a random set of parents that will have a random income level and background, which will effect the players social status and finances.  
    - A wealthy child will not have to get a summertime or after school job and is less likely to deal drugs or entertain other illegal activity as a means of making money.  
4. The player should have some traits that can be chosen before the game starts.  These traits should affect the player's life in a meaningful way.  The player should also be able to make choices that affect their relationships with other characters.  
5. After trait choices are chosen, the player will then be given random traits such as looks, body type, and other physical attributes.  These traits should not be chosen by the player. These will effect their relationship options and opportunities. An unattractive player will have less attention from other characters and less opportunities than an attractive player initially.  The player will need to rely more on personailty and wit, which can be developed by watching different shows and movies and reading books.  They can develop charisma, sense of humor and charm, where as an attractive player doesn't need these as much.  
6. The game should have a day cycle, a school day and practice and/or game. The weeks/months should reflect an actual high schools baseball season. The player will need to balance their school work with their baseball practice and games.  The player should also have time for their relationships with other characters.
7. Each day the player should have the ability to make choices that affect their life.  These choices can range from simple choices like what to wear to more complex choices that affect their relationships with other characters.  The game should also have a system for
keeping track of the player's progress in school.  The player should be able to choose their classes and their teachers.  The player should also be able to choose their extracurricular activities, which can range from healthy to destructive activities.  
8. Relationships should be important in the game.  Players can develop relationships with other characters such as friends, rivals, romantic interests, and enemies.  These relationships should have an effect on the player's life.  For example, a player may have a rival that will try to sabotage their life, or a friend that will help them.  A romantic interest may be a source of support or a source of conflict.  A mentor may provide guidance and support, or a mentor may be a source of conflict.
