Target Market: Fans of OOTP Baseball, Punch Club, New Star Soccer, and Football Manager who want deeper, darker RPG life mechanics.

The Concept: A single-player, time-and-resource management RPG. You start out as a talented but under-resourced high school baseball player and work your way to college, minors and then the MLB. You must manage a rigid 24-hour clock: sleep, school, practice, and games. To afford gear, food, and rent, you choose a path: work a minimum-wage job (safe, low money, costs time/energy) or engage in illicit activities like drug dealing or fencing (fast money, high risk of arrest, injury, or suspension). The UI is split between your "Baseball Dashboard" (stats, scouting reports) and your "Burner Phone / Bank" (managing connections, illicit deals, and basic survival).

You have children that can also become baseball players and this is how the game continues through your offspring.  Otherwise the game ends once no offspring continues their career toward the majors.  

Main goal is to produce as many HOF players through your family as possible.

Vibe Coding Strategy:

Claude Opus 4.8: Acts as the physics and statistics engine. It simulates the baseball games pitch-by-pitch based on your player's current attributes, fatigue levels, and equipment quality.

Claude Fable 5: Drives the narrative "Sims" engine. It generates the complex life events, relationship text messages (girlfriends, coaches, shady contacts), and calculates the RNG consequences of your illegal actions.

Claude Sonnet 5: Codes the extensive frontend UI. We want this to look like a slick hybrid between a banking app, an iMessage screen, and a fantasy sports dashboard.

Asset Pipeline: 100% UI and text. The visual hook relies on dark mode themes, satisfying progress bars, and calendar interfaces. For character contacts, use Midjourney to generate consistent 2D portraits (e.g., prompt: gritty polaroid, mugshot style, 2D indie game portrait).

Steam Compliance: Disclose the pre-generated portraits. You will also need to ensure the game gets an appropriate mature rating on the Steam store due to the drug dealing and gambling references, which is fully supported by Steam's content policies.
