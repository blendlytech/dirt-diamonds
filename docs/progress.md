**What shipped:**

- **`DaySchedule` (new, `Assets/Simulation/Life/DaySchedule.cs`):** readonly struct, five hour blocks (Sleep/School/Practice/Game/Work), ctor-validated ≥0 and ≤24; unallocated hours are "free". Life-folder-local and Data/Baseball-free, so `NeedsDecayHarness`'s wildcard compile stays untouched.
- **`LifeSimManager` avatar split:** `SetAvatar` / `SetTodaySchedule` / `TryGetTodaySchedule` / `ClearTodaySchedule` / `AvatarSchoolAvailable`. With no plan set, the avatar days through the **exact pre-9b `TickHour` loop** — marking an avatar alone changes nothing (the `AutopilotAttendedGames` mirror; headless callers unmodified). A set plan is **one-shot**: consumed by the day it runs, an unplanned tomorrow autopilots (the pending-game-forfeit mirror). `SetAvatar` drops any pending plan (an heir never inherits the retiree's day).
- **Canonical day order — Sleep runs LAST (found the hard way):** first draft ran Sleep first and the harness caught it — sleeping from a full meter burns the whole restore against the 100-clamp, an 8h-sleep day ended at Sleep 33.5. Order is now School → Practice → Game → Work → free hours (standard autopilot, crisis override intact) → Sleep as the night cap (worst end-of-day Sleep 69+ over the scripted week). Manual blocks are uninterruptible (player intent outranks the crisis override in the skeleton — disclosed; the override still governs free hours).
- **School gate:** `SetTodaySchedule` rejects School hours unless `AvatarSchoolAvailable` — a plain bool because the Life assembly must never see `LeagueTier`; the bridge projects `tier is HS or College` into it.
- **`AvatarChangedEvent` (new, CoreEvents.cs):** published by `CareerManager.ActivateAvatar` when a bus is attached (creation + succession); GameManager subscribes and `SyncLifeSimAvatar` re-points the Life sim + school gate. Boot-time load activates before the career attaches, so `_Ready` syncs that case directly. **Side-effect fix of a pre-existing gap:** a mid-session avatar (fresh creation, or an heir born this session) was never seeded into the life sim until next boot — the sync handler now projects its Players row in first (`Seed` is idempotent).
- **Harness additions (the plan's two validation demands):** (1) a never-planned avatar reproduces the pre-9b autopilot trace **bit-for-bit over 30 days** (needs+stress+funds, avatar and bystander, stress-seeded worlds); (2) a fully-allocated 24h day matches a closed-form recomputation of the block math **bit-exactly**; plus a scripted week of manual plans (one-shot consumption daily, bystander NPC bit-identical to a no-avatar control world, night cap keeps Sleep restored, Hygiene off-critical via evening Showers, funds strictly falling proves the free-hour autopilot really acts — blocks never spend).

**Disclosed findings / known artifacts:** a 19h-committed plan **genuinely starves Hunger** (one evening Eat +45 can't outrun ~100/day decay; end-of-day Hunger 0.0 all week) — real tuning reality, deliberately unasserted; 8a/9d should consider meal access on Work/School blocks. Blocks charge no `FinancialCost` and grant no restore except Sleep. The plan is in-memory only (lost on quit — pacing/intent, not sim state). Game hours are life-side inert; coordinating "Game block ⇒ play the pending attended game" is the ScheduleScreen's job against `CareerManager.TryGetPendingGame`.

Deliberately **not modal** like EventChoiceScreen/SuccessionScreen — a plan is optional, so it never joins `AttendedGameScreen`'s day-advance gate — instead it follows `BirthNotificationScreen`'s corner-anchored, non-blocking idiom (bottom-right here, to stay clear of the top bar and the top-right birth banner). ## 8a — Survival Economy + Legal Work: SHIPPED (2026-07-04, Sonnet 5, NOT YET COMMITTED)

**Closes the interleave plan's 8a item end to end.** Planned via full plan-mode first — a Plan-agent pressure test against the live code caught two real bugs before any code was written: (1) a divide-by-zero-shaped NaN hazard if the new per-hour funds math had guarded on `FinancialCost == 0` instead of mirroring the existing `restorePerHour` guard's denominator (`TemporalCostHours > 0`); (2) a genuine same-pump race where a blind daily overwrite of `Players.funds` could silently clobber a gritty event's `AdjustFunds` payout, since `GrittyEventFiredEvent` fires off the dispatcher's own background thread and its `FundsImpulseEvent` follow-up is bus-deferred — it can still be queued (mirror not yet updated) when that day's `PersistLifeSimState` flush runs in the same `DispatchPending()` pump. Both designed out (below) rather than patched after the fact.

**What shipped:**

- **`ActionCatalog.cs`:** two new schedule-block-only `NpcActionId`s (`School`, `LegalWork`) — deliberately excluded from `ActionCatalog.All`/`UtilityCalculator.SelectAction`'s scan, so they can never be spontaneously autopilot-picked. `School` (+3 Hunger/hr over 6h, neutral environment) closes part of 9b's disclosed meal-access gap. `LegalWork` (gritty_events.md's "time-skip abstraction... heavily drains Energy and Fitness for minimal payout" — Sleep is this codebase's only Energy analog): +2 Hunger/hr over 8h, $25/hr income (`FinancialCost` negative = income — documented sign convention, first action to use it), zero risk, heavy Sleep×2/Fitness×1.8 environment drain. $25/hr is a tuned constant, not a design-doc anchor — a broke NPC's own funds-blind free-hour autopilot spend (`SelectAction` only discourages a cost via `FinancialCostScore`, never refuses it) already runs well past the $70/week cost-of-living drain on its own, so Legal Work has to outpace both to make working actually pay off.
- **`LifeSimManager.cs` — the core mechanism:** `NpcRuntime` gained `UnflushedFundsDelta`, touched only by a new `ApplyFundsDelta(npc, delta)` helper (diffs the *clamped* value, not the nominal delta, into the accumulator — a debit that bottoms out at the zero floor must not queue more "debt" than the mirror actually gave up). `ApplyAction`'s autopilot debit and the new `TickBlockHours` funds proration both route through it; `OnFundsImpulse` (gritty events) deliberately does **not** — that portion is already committed to the DB by whoever published the impulse. `TickScheduledDay` swaps School/Work from `ActionCatalog.Idle` to the new real definitions (Practice/Game stay Idle — 9d/CareerManager territory respectively). New recurring cost-of-living drain (`CostOfLivingCadenceDays=7`, `WeeklyCostOfLiving=$70`, one bundled rent+food+gear line item, disclosed simplification) fires **avatar-only** — the pressure test's call: NPCs have no schedule and can never reach `School`/`LegalWork` via autopilot, so a uniform drain would just monotonically zero out all ~817 background NPCs with no validated purpose. New `PeekFundsDelta`/`ClearFundsDelta` accessors (deliberately two separate calls, not one get-and-clear) let `GameManager` defer clearing until after a DB commit succeeds.
- **`GameManager.cs`:** `PersistLifeSimNeeds` renamed `PersistLifeSimState` (now covers Needs+Stress+Funds). Funds route through `Players.AdjustFunds` (atomic delta) — **never** `UpdateFunds` (a blind overwrite) — keeping `AdjustFunds` the sole writer of `Players.funds` end to end, so it can never race a same-pump gritty-event payout (deltas commute; overwrites don't). Accumulators are cleared only **after** `CommitBatch()` succeeds; a rollback leaves them intact so nothing is silently lost to the next attempt.
- **`NeedsDecayHarness` (Data-free — proves the Life-folder mechanism, not the GameManager/DB wiring):** grew 69→**69/69** (net: several new checks, one old assertion replaced). New: a School/LegalWork Environment-range check (they're excluded from the existing `ActionCatalog.All` sweep); the fully-allocated-day closed-form fixture rewritten to derive School's/Work's real restore+environment for their hours instead of assuming all-Idle, plus an exact Funds assertion (3h × $25/hr, zero free-hour noise); the Guarantee-1 "never-planned avatar ≡ plain NPC" 30-day check split into Needs/Stress (still bit-identical) vs. Funds (now diverges from a plain NPC by **exactly** the accumulated cost-of-living total — `4 cadence days × $70 = $280`, proven arithmetically, not just asserted); a new 30-day funds-solvency pair (the plan's explicit ask) — a working avatar (`8 Sleep/8 Work/8 free`) ends **above** its $500 seed ($500→$2,989), a non-working avatar (`8 Sleep/16 free`) ends **below** it ($500→$0).

Proves the fix helps without overclaiming it solves starvation; the underlying reality is unchanged from 9b's original finding, now re-confirmed rather than assumed.
