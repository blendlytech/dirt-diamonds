# Design — Criminal Underworld Content Arc: `DIRT-3` "Product & Turf"

**Author:** Claude Opus 4.8 (narrative architecture) · **Slice:** Content arc, batch 3 of the criminal spine · **Status:** spec written for Sonnet 5 to author `DIRT-3` against; Fable 5 reviews + gates. Companion to `docs/design/criminal_underworld_content_arc.md` (the arc contract + `DIRT-1`) and `docs/design/criminal_underworld_dirt2_the_fix.md` (`DIRT-2`) — read both first — and to `docs/design/hustle_minigames_depth_pass.md` + `docs/design/hustles_narcotics_fencing.md` (the mini-games whose state this batch finally *reads*). Obeys `docs/design/gritty_event_framework.md` (the closed content vocabulary).

This is the promotion of the arc doc's §8 `DIRT-3` outline into a full buildable spec. `DIRT-1` was an authored cascade (a gambling-debt spiral); `DIRT-2` was an authored cascade (the syndicate calls in the marker). **`DIRT-3` is different in kind: it is *reactive* content that fires off the state the player generated by actually playing the hustle mini-games.** The narcotics/fencing/robbery/Hold'em screens are prolific *writers* of durable engine flags that today almost nothing reads and nothing clears. `DIRT-3` is the reader and the clearer — it gives that engine-generated state a narrative life, and in doing so turns the four hustles from isolated silos into a lived-in criminal existence. Product comes back on you; a corner has to be defended; a job that went bad puts you in a cell. This is the batch where **playing the hustles becomes its own on-ramp into the criminal arc.**

---

## 1. Design intent — what "Product & Turf" should feel like

- **The hustles you actually play generate the story.** A player who never opens a hustle screen never sees a single `DIRT-3` beat; a player who runs corners and pulls jobs triggers them organically, off the flags those runs wrote. This is the payoff the depth-pass promised (`hustle_minigames_depth_pass.md` §5.2: *"a future DIRT batch can fire consequences off it"*). The reward for engaging with the mini-games is that the world reacts.
- **Consequence closes a loop the engine left open.** The mini-games set `bad_product`, `controls_turf_local`, `robbery_bust`, `spoiled_goods`, `gambling_bust`, `hot_goods`, `narc_watchlist` — and then, mostly, *nothing happens*. Those flags are written and forgotten (only the shipped `narcotics_arrest` reads one of them). `DIRT-3` is the first content to read the whole set, and the first to **clear** the durable ones — so a bad batch, a botched robbery, a contested corner each get a narrative resolution instead of sitting on the row forever.
- **The teeth are proportionate to the hustle.** Each flag family earns the consequence its fiction implies: **robbery → an arrest absence** (the deferred `robbery_bust` teeth, §2); **turf → an injury absence** (you got jumped defending the corner); **product → reputation, money, and heat** (refunds, a soured block); **the card room → heat and exposure**. Fast money the mini-games already paid out now buys the career-threatening tail the arc's thesis demands.
- **Hustling *is* being in the life.** The meaningful `DIRT-3` beats set the shipped spine flag `in_the_life`, making the mini-games a **third on-ramp** into the whole criminal arc (alongside `DIRT-1`'s bribe and debt). A pure-hustle player who never owed Sal a dime still flows into `dirt_family_finds_out`, `dirt_clean_break`, and — once they make the show — `DIRT-2`'s `dirt_made_useful`. The interactive layer and the narrative layer finally share one state.
- **There is a door out, and it's turf-shaped.** You can give up the corner — the one place content deliberately *writes* a hustle-engine flag (clearing `controls_turf_local`), the mechanical inverse of everywhere else the arc only reads engine state. The full-life exit still routes through `DIRT-1`'s shipped `dirt_clean_break`; `DIRT-3` converges into it rather than duplicating it.

**The deferred seam this batch fulfills.** `hustle_minigames_depth_pass.md` §11 explicitly parked **"Robbery arrest-absence teeth — deferred to the DIRT arc (Opus → Sonnet)"**: the mini-game only *sets* `robbery_bust`; *"a content batch decides its teeth."* `DIRT-3` is that batch, and `dirt_robbery_fallout` is that decision. Call it out in the batch header as the seam being closed.

**Four collisions this batch must route around — all real, all disclosed:**

1. **The flag names drifted; use the shipped ones.** Gate on `controls_turf_local`, **not** `controls_turf_f`/`controls_turf_<n>`. The four newer flags (`hot_goods`, `robbery_bust`, `spoiled_goods`, `gambling_bust`) postdate the arc doc entirely. See collision 2 for the allowlist consequence.
2. **`check_event_graph_integrity` check #2's engine-flag allowlist is stale — one companion edit required.** The skill (`.claude/skills/check_event_graph_integrity/SKILL.md`, last ¶) whitelists only `narc_watchlist`, `bad_product`, `controls_turf_f`/`controls_turf_<n>` as "documented engine-written flags" that a prerequisite may reference without a content `set_flag` (check #2 treats them as pre-satisfied, like `ped_active`). `DIRT-3` gates on **seven** engine flags, four of them not on that list, and the turf one under a name the list gets wrong. **Required companion edit (disclose in the header, Fable-adjacent, zero C#):** update that ¶ to correct `controls_turf_f`/`controls_turf_<n>` → `controls_turf_local` and add `hot_goods`, `robbery_bust`, `spoiled_goods`, `gambling_bust`. This is a documentation edit to the content-validation skill so its knowledge matches shipped `HustleService`; it is **not** an engine change and keeps `DIRT-3` calibration-inert. **If** the `GrittyEventsHarness` also carries an *automated* engine-flag allowlist constant (this pass did not find one — the general orphan rule appears to be skill-manual, and the shipped `narc_watchlist` gate in `roster_availability_events.json` passes today), extend that too; Sonnet verifies during the build by running the harness.
3. **`detection_risk` is still the PED meter, not a heat gauge.** `DIRT-3` continues the arc rule (arc doc §2 collision 1): heat is read via the boolean hustle flags above, **never** via the numeric `detection_risk` field. A hustler who never juiced must never draw the `caught_juicing` "tester with a clipboard" beat. (The mini-games *do* raise `detection_risk` numerically — but content does not read it. The shared-numeric-heat re-gate remains a `DIRT-4` concern.)
4. **There is no `crew` rival target.** The content `relationship` consequence's `target` is a closed set: `teammate|opponent|league|teammate_ex_of_partner` (SKILL.md §"vocabulary"). A turf crew is none of these, so **do not author `relationship kind:rival target:crew`** — it will loader-reject. The arc outline's "a turf dispute that spawns a rival" is satisfied two ways without inventing a target: (a) the *mechanical* on-field rival already exists — `NarcoticsHustle` §3.3 pushes a real Rival edge onto a crew-rep NPC when a turf war is lost, and the Phase-6 transport carries it onto the field for free if that rep is rostered; (b) `DIRT-3`'s turf dispute narrates the antagonism through `person_stat reputation`/`social_status` + `stress` + the injury absence. **Recommended default (locked): narrate via `person_stat`/`stress`/absence; let the mechanical rival come from the hustle engine.** Disclosed alternative if the user wants a content-spawned on-field consequence: a single `relationship kind:rival target:opponent` on the dispute (a crewmate who also plays for a division rival) — flavor-imperfect, Sonnet's call, disclose if used.

## 4. Authoring contract deltas (on top of arc doc §4 + DIRT-2 §4)

Everything in the arc doc §4 holds (closed vocabulary, no engine changes, `scope: avatar` for the descent, `interest` is a no-op on the avatar, flag hygiene, absence semantics, calibration-inert). The `DIRT-3`-specific restatements:

1. **`DIRT-3` introduces ZERO new content flags.** It only (a) *reads* the seven shipped engine flags, (b) *sets* the shipped spine flag `in_the_life`, and (c) *clears* two engine flags on their resolution branches (`robbery_bust`, `controls_turf_local`). This is deliberate minimalism — the engine flags already carry all the state the batch needs, so there is nothing for check #3 (dead-end sets) to catch: the only `set_flag` emitted is `in_the_life`, already read three ways in the shipped arc. If a beat wants a two-step internal cascade, a new content flag is allowed but must be listed in the header with its reader (default: don't).
2. **Content clears engine flags — the one disclosed inverse interaction.** Everywhere else the arc only *gates on* hustle-engine flags. `DIRT-3` breaks that in exactly two safe places: `dirt_robbery_fallout`'s plea clears `robbery_bust`, and `dirt_give_up_the_corner` clears `controls_turf_local`. Both are mechanically safe (`Entity_Flags` is a single table; `SetFlag(..., false, day)` is what `HustleService` itself calls at `:288`) and semantically correct (you served the time; you left the corner). `HustleService.BuildNarcoticsContext` reads `controls_turf_local` as `ownsTurf` (`:115`) — clearing it correctly tells the next narcotics run you no longer hold the corner. Disclose both as intentional cross-system writes.
3. **Durable engine flags never self-clear → every `DIRT-3` event is cooldown-paced (check #4).** Five of the seven flags (`bad_product`, `controls_turf_local`, `spoiled_goods`, `gambling_bust`, and `robbery_bust` on the fight-it branch) are never cleared by their gating event's every branch, so every event gating on them **must** carry an explicit `cooldown_days > 0`. Enumerate the cooldowns in the header exactly as `DIRT-1`/`DIRT-2` did. This is the sole pacing mechanism for the reactive beats — there is no `min_days_since` cascade clock here because the flags are engine-written, not content-written (a `min_days_since` on an engine flag reads its `set_on_day`, which is legal and useful for the entry beats, but the cooldown is the loop-breaker).
4. **No `tier` gate — the hustles are tier-agnostic.** Unlike `DIRT-2` (gated `tier >= 2`), `DIRT-3` fires off hustle state a player can generate at any tier (you can run narcotics/robberies in the HS phase). The teeth *scale* with tier for free (an arrest at tier ≥ 2 benches real pro games; at tier 0 it benches HS games) without a gate. **Fresh-save safety is structural, not belt-and-braces:** every `DIRT-3` gate requires an engine flag that only a *played hustle run* can set, and a day-1/2 avatar has run no hustles — so no `DIRT-3` gate can hold on a fresh save by construction, a stronger guarantee than `DIRT-1`'s `hs_started`+14 anchor or `DIRT-2`'s `tier` floor. The harness still proves it (§7).

### 5.2 Event slots

Contract per slot: gate intent, branch shape, consequence *intent*. Prose, exact numbers, and `prompt`/`label`/`outcome`/`text_message` copy are Sonnet's craft. Contacts per §6: faceless crews/heat/booking = `unknown`; the fence/go-between Sal = `the_bookie` (already shipped); the lawyer beat, if used, = `agent_diaz`.

**TURF (the "& Turf" half — all gate `flag_active controls_turf_local`)**

1. **`dirt_turf_dispute`** — *a rival crew contests the corner.* Gate: `flag_active controls_turf_local, min_days_since ~14`. Contact `unknown`. Cooldown ~30 (neither branch clears the gate). Weight ~0.12.
   - *stand your ground* — `absence reason:"injury", days: 3` (you got jumped — engine computes rust from live `health_ceiling`, **do not author a rating debuff**, SKILL.md), `funds −` (medical/protection), `stress +`, `person_stat morality −`, `set_flag in_the_life`. Turf's mechanical teeth; the crew rep's on-field rival, if any, already came from the hustle engine (§2 collision 4).
   - *cede the block* — `person_stat reputation −`, `person_stat social_status −`, `stress −small`, `set_flag in_the_life`. You keep your health and lose face; `controls_turf_local` stays (the corner is still nominally yours; the next narcotics run reads it) → cooldown-paced.
2. **`dirt_the_corner_earns`** — *the territory pays, and demands defense* (the narrative face of the deferred passive-turf-income hook, `hustles_narcotics_fencing.md` §8 — a face without building the recurring tick). Gate: `flag_active controls_turf_local, min_days_since ~10`. Contact `unknown` / `the_bookie`. Cooldown ~21. Weight ~0.12.
   - *collect and reinvest / scale up* — `funds +`, `stress +small`, `person_stat morality −`, `set_flag in_the_life` (moving weight — the deeper-in choice).
   - *keep it quiet* — small `funds +`, `stress −small`, `set_flag in_the_life` (steady, lower exposure).
3. **`dirt_give_up_the_corner`** — *the turf-shaped door out.* Gate: `flag_active controls_turf_local, min_days_since ~30`. Contact `coach_reyes` / `family_mom`. Cooldown ~30. Weight ~0.08.
   - *walk away from the corner* — **`clear_flag controls_turf_local`** (§4.2 — the next narcotics run reads you as no longer holding turf), `stress −`, `person_stat morality +`. A real exit from the turf life; the *full*-life exit (clearing `in_the_life`) still routes through the shipped `dirt_clean_break` — disclose that `DIRT-3` converges its exit rather than duplicating it.
   - *one more season on top* — `funds +small`, `person_stat morality −`, keeps `controls_turf_local` (the pull to hold the corner; cooldown re-offers). Mirrors `DIRT-1`'s `dirt_clean_break`/*one last score*.

**PRODUCT (the narcotics/fencing fallout half)**

4. **`dirt_bad_batch_comes_back`** — *your poison soured the block.* Gate: `flag_active bad_product, min_days_since ~10`. Contact `unknown`. Cooldown ~30 (`bad_product` never clears — cooldown is the only pacing). Weight ~0.12. The narrative teeth for a narcotics bad batch, which today writes the flag and vanishes.
   - *make it right (refunds/payback)* — `funds −`, `person_stat reputation −small`, `stress +`, `set_flag in_the_life`.
   - *deny it's yours* — `person_stat morality −`, `person_stat reputation −`, `stress +`, `set_flag in_the_life`. Cheaper now, worse for the name.
5. **`dirt_lying_low`** — *heat / paranoia texture* (reads the transient watchlist). Gate: `flag_active narc_watchlist, min_days_since ~7`. Contact `unknown`. Cooldown ~21. Weight ~0.10. **Shared read with the shipped `narcotics_arrest`** (§8.1) — a soft reaction to the same heat, not a competitor for a resolution.
   - *lie low for a while* — `stress −`, small `funds −` (lost earning days). Does **not** clear `narc_watchlist` (the engine/arrest owns clearing it — §8.1). `set_flag in_the_life`.
   - *keep pushing* — `funds +small`, `person_stat morality −`, `stress +`, `set_flag in_the_life`.
6. **`dirt_dead_weight`** — *product/goods you couldn't move* (light texture; reads `spoiled_goods`, giving that flag its first reader). Gate: `flag_active spoiled_goods, min_days_since ~10`. Contact `unknown` / `the_bookie`. Cooldown ~30. Weight ~0.08. Minor by design — completes the "all seven hustle flags now have a narrative reader" property.
   - *cut your losses* — small `funds −`, `stress +small`, `set_flag in_the_life`.
   - *dump it and move on* — `stress −small` (no funds). A clean small out.

**ROBBERY / HOT GOODS (the highest teeth)**

7. **`dirt_robbery_fallout`** ★ — *the job that went bad catches up* (**the keystone — fulfills the deferred `robbery_bust` arrest-absence seam, §2**). Gate: `flag_active robbery_bust, min_days_since ~10`. Contact `unknown` (booking, faceless — mirrors `DIRT-1`'s `dirt_busted`) / `agent_diaz` (the lawyer, if a pro-era voice is wanted). Cooldown ~30 (fight-it keeps `robbery_bust`). Weight ~0.10. This is where a robbery finally threatens the diamonds. Tier-agnostic — the arrest benches whatever games exist (§4.4).
   - *take the plea* — `absence reason:"arrest", days: 3`, `funds −` (bail/lawyer), **`clear_flag robbery_bust`** (§4.2 — you served it), `person_stat reputation −`, `stress +`, `set_flag in_the_life`. The paid exit; mirrors `dirt_busted`'s plea verbatim.
   - *fight it* — `absence reason:"arrest", days: 4`, bigger `funds −`, keeps `robbery_bust` (the case grinds on; cooldown re-offers), `stress +big`, `set_flag in_the_life`.
8. **`dirt_sitting_on_hot_goods`** — *move it or it's a liability* (the robbery→fence economy spine, `hustle_minigames_depth_pass.md` §5.2). Gate: `flag_active hot_goods`. Contact `the_bookie`. Nudges the player toward the Fencing screen's `FreshFromAJob` acquisition (which consumes `hot_goods`); this beat **reads but never clears** it — the fence consumes it (§8.2 shared-flag interaction).
   - *take it to Sal* — narrative push to fence (small `funds +` finder's-fee flavor or none — the real money is the fencing screen's), `person_stat morality −`, `set_flag in_the_life`.
   - *sit on it* — `stress +` (it's a liability in your closet), no funds. Keeps `hot_goods`.

**CARD ROOM (Hold'em heat)**

9. **`dirt_card_room_raided`** — *the game you played got raided; you're a known face.* Gate: `flag_active gambling_bust, min_days_since ~7`. Contact `unknown`. Cooldown ~30 (`gambling_bust` never clears). Weight ~0.08. Gives the Hold'em raid flag its first narrative reader — heat/exposure texture, not new teeth.

### 5.3 Pacing & load-order note

Same discipline as `DIRT-1`/`DIRT-2` (arc doc §5.3): the underworld batch sorts after the weight-1.0 onboarding chains, and the per-subject one-fire-per-day cap + file-order tiebreak mean those always win their slots. No `DIRT-3` weight exceeds 0.12. **The fresh-save guarantee is structural** (§4.4): every gate requires an engine flag a played hustle run must set, which a day-1/2 avatar cannot have — so no `DIRT-3` gate holds on a fresh save at any day, without needing `DIRT-1`'s `hs_started` anchor or `DIRT-2`'s tier floor. The `min_days_since` values keep each reactive beat off the heels of the run that armed its flag.
